Education and Student Life

Cluj-Napoca is the most prestigious university center in Romania with 10 universities where approximately 100,000 Romanian and international students study, bringing their youthful energy to the city and making it so vibrant. Find out more about the higher education in the city and what facilities and opportunities are available for students, as well as some insides about the entertainment side of it all.

Universities

Babeș-Bolyai University

Babeș-Bolyai University, established in 1581, holds the highest global ranking among higher education institutions in Romania. The university boasts a diverse curriculum available in Romanian, Hungarian, German, English, Italian, and French.

Across its 22 faculties, which encompass disciplines such as Mathematics and Computer Science, Physics, Chemistry and Chemical Engineering, Biology and Geology, Geography, Environmental Science and Engineering, Law, Letters, History and Philosophy, Sociology and Social Work, Psychology and Educational Sciences, Economic Sciences and Business Management, European Studies, Business, Political, Administrative and Communication Sciences, Physical Education and Sports, Orthodox Theology, Greek Catholic Theology, Reformed Theology and Music, Roman Catholic Theology, Theater and Film, and Engineering, the university offers:

  • 250 undergraduate programs, including 160 in Romanian, 67 in Hungarian, 9 in German, 14 in English, and 1 in French;
  • 253 master's programs across 42 fields, distributed as follows: 162 in Romanian, 46 in Hungarian, 6 in German, 35 in English, 3 in French, and 1 in Italian;
  • 32 doctoral programs spanning various fields.

"Iuliu Hațieganu" University of Medicine and Pharmacy

The "Iuliu Hațieganu" University of Medicine and Pharmacy stands out as the top-rated medical university in Romania, providing academic programs in Romanian, English, and French. With a diverse student body, the university hosts over 2,500 international students. Its comprehensive educational offerings include bachelor's, master's, doctoral, residency, and ongoing medical training programs.

Within the three faculties of UMF, prospective students have the opportunity to pursue studies in:

  • Faculty of Medicine - Offering 4 undergraduate degree programs:
    • Medicine - a 6-year university program;
    • Nursing - a 4-year university program;
    • Radiology and Medical Imaging - a 3-year university program;
    • Balneophysiokinetotherapy and Recovery Medical - a 3-year university program.
  • Faculty of Dental Medicine - Featuring 2 undergraduate degree programs:
    • Medicine – a 6-year university program;
    • Dental Technology – a 3-year university program.
  • Faculty of Pharmacy - Providing 2 undergraduate study programs:
    • Pharmacy – a 5-year university program;
    • Nutrition and Dietetics – a 3-year university program.

Technical University of Cluj-Napoca

The Technical University of Cluj-Napoca, distinguished for its advanced research and educational endeavors, has firmly established itself as a venerable institution with both national and international acclaim. Currently boasting an enrollment of over 22,000 students, the university offers a comprehensive array of 205 bachelor's, master's, and doctoral programs. Instruction is seamlessly delivered in Romanian as well as in global languages such as English, German, and French.

The University of Cluj-Napoca discreetly houses faculties spanning a diverse spectrum, including Architecture and Urban Planning, Automation and Computers, Road Vehicles, Mechatronics and Mechanics, Construction, Electronics, Telecommunications and Information Technology, Materials and Environmental Engineering, Installations Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Industrial Engineering, and the intricate realms of Robotics and Production Management.

University of Agricultural Sciences and Veterinary Medicine

The legacy of the esteemed Cluj-Napoca Higher School of Agriculture, founded in October 1869, lives on in the University of Agricultural Sciences and Veterinary Medicine. This institution proudly extends its educational offerings in Romanian, English, and French.

Comprising five faculties—Agriculture, Horticulture, Animal Husbandry and Biotechnologies, Food Science and Technology, and Veterinary Medicine—the university invites students to delve into a diverse array of academic pursuits. With 28 bachelor's programs, 27 master's programs, and a plethora of doctoral fields, the university provides a fertile ground for scholarly exploration and growth.

University of Art and Design

Since its inception in 1926, the University of Art and Design of Cluj-Napoca (UAD) has solidified its position as a paramount institution in the realm of visual arts, both nationally and internationally, particularly in South-Eastern Europe.

The university, a beacon for creative minds, presents a diverse array of academic offerings in the field of visual arts. Divided into three cycles, the academic landscape unfolds with ten bachelor's degree programs in Cycle I, followed by nine master's university study programs in Cycle II. For those aspiring to reach the pinnacle of artistic scholarship, Cycle III offers a doctoral university study program, inviting individuals to embark on an enriching journey of advanced research and creative exploration.

"Gheorghe Dima" Music Academy

Renowned for its vibrancy in musical education, the "Gheorghe Dima" Music Academy stands as one of Romania's dynamic higher education institutions, dedicated to nurturing excellence in musical creation, interpretation, pedagogy, and musicology. The academy extends a comprehensive educational spectrum, encompassing bachelor's, master's, and doctoral programs across its two faculties.

Within the Faculty of Music Interpretation, students can immerse themselves in a four-year bachelor's program, with diverse options such as Musical Performance on various instruments including stringed instruments (violin, viola, cello, double bass, classical guitar, harp), wind and percussion instruments (flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon, horn, trumpet, trombone, tuba, saxophone, percussion), and keyboard instruments (piano, organ, harpsichord). Additionally, students may choose paths in Musical Interpretation with a focus on Canto or explore the realm of Musical Performing Arts.

The Theoretical Faculty, with its specialized research directions, delves into Composition (musical creation), Conducting (musical interpretation), Musicology (musicological research), Music Education and Teaching, as well as the innovative intersections of Information and Communication Technologies in the musical field and Folklore. This distinctive approach positions the academy at the forefront of musical education, fostering both artistic mastery and scholarly inquiry.

Private Universities

In Cluj-Napoca, other universities, which are private, include "Dimitrie Cantemir" Christian University, "Bogdan-Vodă" University, the Protestant Theological Institute (ITP), and Sapientia University. However, Sapientia University is distinctive among them as the sole institution offering studies in Hungarian and German through its Faculty of Sciences and Arts.

Student Life

Volunteering. Student Organizations.

Volunteering as a student is a powerful way to make a real impact while gaining practical experience. It goes beyond the classroom, offering a chance to connect with diverse communities, develop valuable skills, and create lasting memories. The journey of giving back not only enriches your academic years but also shapes you into a more compassionate and socially conscious individual. There are plenty of options to choose from in Cluj:

  • BEST Cluj-Napoca (Board of European Students of Technology) - An international-focused organization with over 80 Local BEST Groups across Europe, organizes events for tech students such as IT and engineering competitions, career fairs, season courses in various domains etc.;
  • OSUT Cluj-Napoca (Organization of Students of the Technical University) - Representative Organization for the students of TUCN;
  • OSUBB (Organization of Students of the Babeș-Bolyai University);
  • OspN (Organization of Students for Nature);
  • ESN Cluj-Napoca (Erasmus Student Network);
  • IEEE Student Branch Cluj-Napoca (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers);
  • AIESEC - An International Organization helping young people discover their leadership potential and what domain would suit them best;
  • Beard Brothers - From planting trees to helping educate children, they fight to change mentalities regarding social issues and more;
  • Digital Kids - Giving children access to and educating them on how to use the internet responsibly and for learning purposes,

    • and many, many more.

Sports

There are several sports facilities in the city, where you can find football, basketball, tennis fields and more. Some of the sports centers in the city are: "Unirea", "Gheorgheni", "La Terenuri", "Napoca" Sports Complexes.

If you like swimming, you might want to try the Swimming Pool of the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca. It is dedicated to a large public, from children, to students, undergraduates, sportsmen, adults, as it is one of the most agreeable place for sports and relaxation in our city. The public can use the swimming pools according to the timetable, either by paying their tickets or by subscription. The university staff and students benefit from a reduction of the tickets price. However, you can find more nice swimming pools in the city.

If you are a climbing junkie, you'll be happy to know that you can find a handful of climbing gyms in Cluj, such as Gravity, Centrala de Escaladă and Skai Urban Craig. Be on the lookout, because they sometimes organize different workshops, competitions and outdoor activities as well.

If you want a taste of adventure in open air, check out Adrenalin Park for some interesting adventure trails.

Additionally, there are a few of entities providing services such as paragliding, rafting, canyoning, via ferrata, hiking outings, outdoor climbing and so on. Some of these entities areTransylvania Extreme Adventures, Apuseni Mountain Club, Romanian Alpine Club.

Dining

If you prefer eating out rather than cooking, Cluj comes with a great deal of cafeterias, restaurants and pubs. The most convenient places to dine are, of course, the cafeterias, since they are more affordable, but there are also some not so costly restaurants and pubs. You can find a list with all the restaurants, pubs and cafes in Cluj on Tripadvisor.

Accommodation

Renting an apartment in Cluj can be rather tricky for a student's wallet. Moreover, living on campus provides opportunities to build a strong sense of community and engage in social activities, as well as increased proximity to some classes, libraries and cafeterias. Luckily, in Cluj-Napoca you can find several student campuses providing accommodation, such as:

  • "Observator" Student Complex of the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca;
  • Mărăști Student Complex of the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca;
  • Hașdeu Student Complex of Babeș-Bolyai University;
  • Agronomia Student Complex of University of Agricultural Sciences and Veterinary Medicine;

and so on.
