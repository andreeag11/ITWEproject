Selected sightseeing

There is plenty to see and do in the city. From churches in various architectural styles to unique museums, an impressive amount of green areas for such a big city and much more, check out the objectives we selected for you below.

Unirii Square. St. Michael's Church. National Art Museum.

The heart of the city is Piața Unirii – the Union Square – a beautiful square where you will find quite a few interesting objectives. This is also where the Christmas Fair is held every year, as well as the Transylvania International Film Festival and other cultural events too.

St. Michael's Church was built way back in 14th century and is probably one of the most impressive Gothic churches in Transylvania, inside and out. It features one of the tallest towers in the city, soaring above Cluj's cityscape at more than 76 meters high. You can also get up to the top of the tower for an incredible view of the city from above.

Cross the road and you can also visit the National Art Museum of Cluj-Napoca, which housed an impressive collection of art, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts by some of Romania's most renowned artists as well as international artists.

Museum Square. Fransican Monastery. Transylvanian History Museum.

Pretty close to Unirii Square, you can find Museum Square, with many buildings of a beautiful architecture. Here you can visit the Franciscan Monastery and the Transylvanian History Museum. On the way there you will pass the memorial house where king Matthias Corvinus was born.

Avram Iancu Square.
National Opera House.

Another important square is Avram Iancu, where you can find an Orthodox Cathedral and, more importantly, you can also appreciate the beauty and maybe pay a visit to the Romanian National Opera and Theater. The building boasts a seating capacity of over 900 people and was designed in the Neo-baroque architectural style. Its features some inflections inspired by Art Nouveau, which add to the overall beauty and grandeur of the space.

At more or less one or two hundred meters from here, you can find Tailors’ Tower, a well-preserved historical monument that used to be part of the medieval fortifications of the city way back in the 15th Century when it was built. Inside, you will also find a cultural space with an exhibition and a gallery where you can learn more about the history of the city.

Potaissa Street. Cluj Citadel Walls.

To transport yourself to medieval times, stroll down Potaissa Street in Cluj, where various events like flower markets, wine festivals, and medieval reenactments take place.

Potaissa Street, situated in the medieval heart of Cluj, is a captivating experience for those yearning for a journey into the past. It features one of the remaining sections of the medieval defensive walls of Cluj's old citadel. Moreover, it offers a gateway to a realm of fairy tales, with enchanting knights, ladies, and true heroes depicted in the Medieval Camps. Picture people in period attire, captivating fire shows, archery contests, and live battles – a dreamlike experience for both children and adults!

"Simion Bărnuțiu" Central Park. Casino.

Covering an area of 7 hectares, the park boasts a range of trees, plants, and flowers, as well as walking trails, benches, and a handful of attractions, which we will tell you about in a minute.

One of the main attractions of the park is its serene lake, home to a variety of waterfowl and other wildlife. You can rent paddle boats and enjoy a leisurely ride on the lake while taking in the beautiful surroundings or, if you visit in the winter and the weather is cold enough, you can try ice-skating with the locals. There is also an area for picnicking or hammocking, as well as a restaurant right by the lake.

While you are exploring Central Park "Simion Bărnuțiu", be sure to drop by and check out the Casino, a beautiful well-preserved historical building constructed in the early 20th century, considered a prime example of Art Nouveau architecture in Transylvania.

While in the past it was used as a casino, then a hospital, then the Communist Party’s headquarters, nowadays the building often hosts a variety of events, including art exhibitions, theater performances, and concerts.

"Cetățuia" Park

If you're looking for the best viewpoint in the city, this is it. The park is situated on the site of a former fortress on a hill overlooking the entire city, offering a stunning view of Cluj and its surroundings from above. You will have to walk uphill for about 10 minutes, but it is well worth it. Be sure to check it out after sunset for an even more spectacular view.

"Alexandru Borza" Botanical Garden

Founded in 1920, the Botanical Garden is a scientific, didactic and educational institution, whose foundation was laid by Professor Alexandru Borza.

Within its grounds, the garden boasts over 10,000 plant species sourced from various corners of the globe, organized into distinct sectors such as ornamental, phytogeographic, systematic, economic, and medicinal. Representing the rich biodiversity of Romanian flora and vegetation, the collection encompasses plants from the Transylvanian plains, Carpathian Mountains, Banat, and other regions.

Key highlights of the garden include:

  • the Japanese Garden, designed in the traditional "gyo-no-niwa" style and featuring a creek and a Japanese-style cottage;
  • the Roman Garden, where the statue of the goddess Ceres presides over archaeological remnants from the ancient Roman colony Napoca;
  • the Botanical Museum housing around 7,000 exhibits;
  • and a greenhouse complex consisting of six sections: the aquarium greenhouse, palm trees greenhouse, Mediterranean and Australian plants greenhouse, succulent plants greenhouse, Bromeliaceae greenhouse, and orchids and ferns greenhouse.

Etnographic Museum of Transylvania and "Romulus Vuia" National Ethnographic Park

The Etnographic Museum of Transylvania was established in 1922 and in 1929 it expanded with the addition of an open-air exhibition, the first of its kind in Romania, named the "Romulus Vuia" National Ethnographic Park.

The current permanent exhibition, titled "Folk Culture in Transylvania - 18th to 20th Century," was inaugurated in 2006. It delves into the primary realms of material and spiritual culture in rural Transylvania, showcasing representative artifacts carefully selected from the museum's extensive collection of over 40,000 items.

The exhibition encompasses various aspects, including podotactile bands designed for the visually impaired, displays featuring traditional harvest wreaths, sections dedicated to Jewish ceremonial items, and exhibits portraying themes of birth, childhood, and youth.

Steampunk Transylvania Museum

If you are looking for a fun and interactive modern art museum to visit in Cluj, we highly recommend you check out Steampunk Transylvania.

The museum delves into the Steampunk subculture, fusing Victorian-era aesthetics with Transylvania's gothic and folkloric traditions. Throughout the entirely Steampunk-themed space, you'll encounter captivating artworks crafted from brass, copper machinery, leather, and lace clothing. Inspired by Transylvanian folklore and history, these pieces create a dark and mysterious ambiance that captures the imagination and leaves a lasting impression.

Forests. Hiking trails.

Cluj-Napoca is surrounded by a diverse and scenic landscape that provides excellent opportunities for hiking enthusiasts.

Some options are the forests Hoia Baciu (which is also internationally renowed for its supernatural oddities) and Făget, which are partly in the city. Moreover, you can discover plenty of hiking trails around Cluj on Munții-Noștri.ro (Our Mountains).

Accommodation

There are plenty of hotels and other accommodation facillities in Cluj-Napoca. Most of them are documented on the Booking.com site or, if you are looking for cheaper deals, you can also check out the Airbnb website.

Transportation

There are plenty of available modes of transport in Cluj-Napoca - we recommend the most sustainable ones, since the car traffic is often heavy, especially during peak hours.

Public Transportation

Cluj-Napoca has an extensive network of buses and trolleybuses that cover various routes within the city and its surroundings. The public transportation system is a convenient and affordable way to travel.

You can find all lines on the local transportation authority's website.

Bike-Friendly City

Although there is always room for improvement, Cluj-Napoca is known for being bicycle-friendly, with dedicated bike lanes and bike-sharing programs. Many residents use bicycles as an eco-friendly and healthy means of commuting, and there are also cycling events and initiatives to promote sustainable transportation.

Taxi Services

Taxis are readily available in Cluj-Napoca and can be hailed on the street or booked through various taxi companies. They offer a more flexible and direct mode of transportation, especially for those who prefer door-to-door service.

Ride-Sharing Services

Ride-sharing services, such as Uber, operate in Cluj-Napoca, providing an additional option for convenient and on-demand transportation.

Car Rentals

Car rental services are available for those who prefer the flexibility of driving themselves. Rental agencies offer a range of vehicles, and driving in and around Cluj-Napoca is generally manageable.

Rail Transportation

Cluj-Napoca has a railway station that connects the city to other major cities in Romania and Europe. The railway system provides an alternative for those who prefer train travel.

"Avram Iancu" International Airport

The city is served by Cluj-Napoca International Airport (Avram Iancu International Airport), which is the second-busiest airport in Romania.
The "Avram Iancu" airport connects Cluj-Napoca to various domestic and international destinations, facilitating air travel.

..or visit the surroundings!

If you have a bit more time to get around, we recommend you to visit Turda, Rimetea village, the Alba-Iulia Fortress and maybe even the Corvin Castle. For the time being, allow us to tell you more about two particular locations at a distance of less than an hour by car.

Turda Salt Mine

Turda Salt Mine is one of the most spectacular tourist destinations in the world.

It is the underground formation that people created in a special natural environment, in the depths of Transylvania, digging into the salt deposited after the evaporation of the sea that covered the entire region millions of years ago. Now, the salt from Turda Salt Mine could cover the salt requirement for the entire Planet for 60 years, if it were necessary.

Turda Salt Mine is located in Turda, still a part of Cluj county, at a distance of 31km from Cluj-Napoca, so it's definitely worth paying it a visit if you have more time to spare.

Turda Gorges

Carved by the erosion of Jurassic limestone, the canyon stretches for 2,900 meters, with towering walls reaching heights of up to 300 meters. The entire surface area of the canyon spans 324 hectares.

Home to over 1,000 plant and animal species, including rare or endangered varieties like wild garlic and certain eagle species, the canyon is a biodiversity hotspot.
