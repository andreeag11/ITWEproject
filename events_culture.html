Events and Culture

Events and culture

Cluj-Napoca's cultural tapestry unfolds with plenty of cinemas, libraries, and art spaces. Cinemas bring diverse film experiences, libraries serve as intellectual retreats or study hubs, and various cultural venues contribute to the city's dynamic and varied cultural identity.

Events

The city hosts a dynamic array of events, ranging from film festivals to music gatherings. These diverse cultural offerings include renowned international festivals and local performances, making the city a vibrant hub for entertainment and celebration.

Transilvania International
Film Festival

Established in 2002, the Transilvania International Film Festival (TIFF) stands as Romania's first and largest international film festival. Attracting over 100,000 participants annually, TIFF is a significant cultural event in the region. Hosted in Cluj-Napoca, a city with both modern amenities and a rich history, the festival earned the city a spot in the UNESCO City of Film network in 2021.

Accessible to the general public and industry professionals, TIFF presents a diverse program of over 250 films, including cine-concerts, concerts, art exhibitions, and opportunities for filmmakers to connect with Romanian audiences. The festival is dedicated to discovering new voices in cinema, featuring international competitions for first and second-time directors in both fiction and documentary categories. It also highlights contemporary Romanian cinema through its Romanian Days showcase.

TIFF's program extends to screening highly anticipated films, provocative productions, and includes sections focused on children and film education (EducaTIFF), as well as retrospectives of renowned filmmakers from around the world.

Comedy Cluj
International Festival

The largest comedy film festival in Europe, Comedy Cluj International Festival, showcases over 100 films during its 10-day run, featuring screenings, industry meet-ups, public interactions, special events, and cocktails. It provides a prime opportunity to connect with fellow comedy creators, engage with an audience passionate about the genre, and network with influential figures in the industry. The inaugural edition of Comedy Cluj took place in 2009, featuring 70 films from 19 countries, along with workshops, street theater performances, conferences, and parties. The festival encompasses both competitive and non-competitive sections.

Electric Castle

Nestled near the iconic 15th-century Bánffy Castle in Transylvania, Electric Castle is a music festival renowned for its creativity, offering diverse lineups, new media installations, and engaging talks. Surrounded by stunning scenery, the festival provides a unique way of living and feeling.

With round-the-clock entertainment, artistic performances, immersive installations, stand-up comedy, a fashion and fair area, and diverse food and drink vendors, Electric Castle ensures continuous excitement.

Over its nine years of existence, the festival has welcomed internationally acclaimed music artists such as Gorillaz, Twenty One Pilots, Florence + The Machine, Macklemore, Iggy Pop, Jessie J, Skrillex, The Prodigy, Die Antwoord, deadmau5, Thirty Seconds To Mars, Bring Me The Horizon and more.

UNTOLD Festival

In Transylvania, UNTOLD Festival is all about music and magic. Picture this: beats turning into symphonies, nights filled with vibrant melodies. No strict schedule, just endless possibilities. Once you're in, you're part of the action. Thematic stages become gateways to unique auditory journeys—from electronic beats to live performances, they've got it all. Think Armin van Buuren, Martin Garrix, David Guetta—the big names amplifying the vibe. It's not just a festival; it's a music experience where energy is real, and the sound is everything.

Jazz in the Park

Founded in 1929, the Ethnographic Park (or "Village Museum") in Cluj-Napoca, transforms into a vibrant hub during the festival, hosting stages, restaurants, and various activities.

Limited to 7,000 participants daily, Jazz in the Park turns the village into a lively space with 40 concerts across 4 stages, 6 restaurants, workshops, relaxation areas, and craft fairs. It aims to promote positive values in the community through music and art, setting an example for a happier and more caring world.

Jazz was chosen as the festival's ambassador for its unique ability to encourage introspection. A timeless genre, it adds a splash of color to the city's cultural agenda, making attendees think and connect with themselves. The team loves the festival and invites everyone to discover its unique charm.

Smida Jazz Festival

Immerse yourself in three days of tranquility at Smida Jazz Festival, nestled in the Apuseni Mountains, just a scenic drive from Cluj-Napoca. Surrounded by nature's serenade—birdsong, Beliș Lake, and forest tranquility—you'll discover a haven to rejuvenate your senses.

During the day, explore the village's forests on guided walks or bike tours, indulge in water activities like kayaking on Beliș Lake, or engage in the Grow Area. This dynamic space offers creative workshops in writing, music, and photography, along with insightful talks on mental health, civic rights, education, and journalism. Alternatively, simply unwind in a hammock beneath an old tree, listening to the melodies of Smida birds. Children have their special playground in the Kids Area.

The festival, a short distance from Cluj-Napoca, promises a holistic experience where nature, music, and community seamlessly converge.

Cluj Musical Autumn

Cluj Musical Autumn stands as Romania's foremost international classical music festival, second only to the biannual "George Enescu" international festival. Since its inaugural edition in 1965, with only a brief interruption, the festival has maintained an annual rhythm.

Known for its diverse musical offerings, the festival presents a range of symphonic, vocal-symphonic concerts, chamber, and soloist recitals spanning the entire history of classical music. This eclectic approach embraces both established names in the national and international music scene and provides a platform for emerging talents. Reflecting a commitment to cultural diversity and intercultural dialogue, the festival has expanded its scope in recent years, incorporating jazz concerts, traditional music, film scores, fusion, and world music.

"George Enescu" Festival

The "George Enescu" Festival, named after the celebrated Romanian composer and violinist, George Enescu, is a distinguished celebration of classical music primarily held in Bucharest. Occasionally, this festival takes its exceptional concerts and events on the road, bringing world-class performances to various cities across Romania, including Cluj-Napoca.

Clujotronic

The Clujotronic journey began in 2012, establishing a platform for exploring innovative intersections of art and technology. Organized by the German Cultural Center and the French Institute in Cluj-Napoca, this annual electro arts festival unites artists and experts from France, Germany, and Romania. Through immersive experiences, performances, installations, game exhibitions, workshops, and talks, Clujotronic fosters interaction among artists, tech developers, and the wider public, shaping a space for digital culture dialogue.

The festival, held at diverse Cluj-Napoca venues like The Paintbrush Factory and Tranzit House, encourages fresh perspectives on urban spaces as vital settings for artistic expression. Embracing experimentation, Clujotronic invites participants to delve into the cultural transformations of the technological era, fostering engagement in collaborative projects and virtual adventures.

Lights On Festival

Lights On is an annual light-art festival occurring in November/December. It introduces artistic light installations in open spaces, guiding people to discover the city's remarkable places through sculptural creations. The event serves as a way of connecting individuals with contemporary art and is made accessible to the public through the support of public and private institutions.

The festival relies on attracting grant funding and partnerships with companies that share the vision of transforming spaces through art. Recognizing the transformative power of art on mindsets and environments, Lights On actively supports local artists by commissioning large-scale works based on their ideas, ensuring these visions come to life.

The Hungarian Cultural Days

The Hungarian Cultural Days in Cluj, Transylvania's largest cultural festival, showcases Hungarian cultural life in Transylvania and the Carpathian Basin. With diverse programs and celebrating its tenth edition, the event serves as a cultural tour de force, demonstrating the vitality of the value-creating community.

As members of the Treasured Cluj Association, they present their culture to the Romanian community, aiming for mutual understanding and highlighting Cluj's diverse and promising development. HCDC's success over the years, serving as a cultural model for other towns in Transylvania, reaffirms its role as a significant cultural event in the Carpathian Basin. The tenth edition features an ever-improving program offering quality entertainment in various city squares, institutions, and less-frequented locations, celebrating the city and its inhabitants.

Transylvania International
Spoken Word Festival

This year, the Transylvania International Spoken Word Festival took place from October 19, featuring four days of captivating spoken word poetry. In a groundbreaking collaboration, three communities — Decopertat, Nest, and Poethree —joined forces to showcase the creative prowess of Cluj-Napoca, highlighting the power of our voices and the potential of spoken word poetry.

The festival brought together over 200 local and international poets, artists, and guests who shared their stories through 12 events across five distinct stages. The diverse program included showcases, street poetry, open mics, art expos, poetry and music improv, workshops, single and team slams, and a main performance that enriched both the spirit and mind.

The festival is at the beginning, but we hope it will take place for many more years to come.

European Researchers' Night

European Researchers' Night is an annual event across Europe, typically held on the last Friday of September. It aims to bring researchers closer to the public by offering hands-on experiments, workshops, and interactive exhibits. The event encourages engagement with science, fostering a better understanding of research and innovation's impact on daily life. Supported by the European Commission, it provides a unique opportunity for the public to interact with researchers and explore the world of scientific discovery.

Museums' Night

Museums' Night is an occasion on which museums and cultural institutions keep their gates open for the public during extended hours, featuring special exhibitions, guided tours, interactive activities, and performances.

Visitors can enjoy an exceptional and diverse program featuring exhibitions of modern art, old Romanian books, classic and contemporary painting, sculpture, photography, minerals from major mining regions, live animals (fish, reptiles), and stuffed wildlife exhibits. The offerings may extend to sports displays, firefighting equipment, and various other surprises.

Cluj Pride

PRIDE Romania is an LGBTQ+ organization committed to promoting human rights, diversity, social inclusion, and anti-discrimination in Romanian civil society.

They organize Cluj Pride, the first event of its kind in Transylvania since 2017. The initiative aims to enhance visibility for the community, encourage participation in various activities, and contribute to the national LGBTQ+ dialogue. This event, featuring themed presentations, film screenings, interactive exhibitions, and community picnics, provides a vibrant local perspective. At the core of Cluj Pride is the Pride March, a powerful demonstration of unity and advocacy that amplifies the voices of the community.

Others

In addition to the ones mentioned, Cluj hosts a vibrant array of many other events throughout the year.

  • Poetry Nights are organised regularly by Decopertat, NEST and Poethree Collective,, in the form of Open Mics, Poetry Slams and sometimes shows.
  • Moreover, there are almost always concerts at one or more of the many venues the city, you just have to keep an eye out for them.
  • If you’re keen, you may wish to consider attending a theatre play, a dance performance, or perhaps go to the opera.
  • Another yearly festivity is Cluj Days, a dynamic celebration showcasing the city's cultural heritage through events, performances, and exhibitions.
  • The Christmas Fair transforms Cluj into a winter wonderland during the holiday season, featuring traditional stalls with handcrafted gifts, festive treats, and warm beverages, being held in Unirii Square.
  • Alongside these, the Street Food Festival tantalizes taste buds with culinary delights.
  • Last but not least, the Central Park Wine Festival adds a sophisticated touch to the city's event calendar.

We covered many of them, but still, the city being so big and populous and important in Transylvania, we cannot possibly cover everything. There are many more gatherings all year round, providing residents and visitors with a rich tapestry of entertainment and cultural exploration.

Cinemas

Cinema ARTA

The history of ARTA cinema is more than the story of a place; it mirrors the events of the past century, intersecting with the city's history, film evolution, and personal microhistories. Notable figures from Transylvanian cinema, such as Janovics Jenő, were linked to its early days.

Despite political regime-induced name changes, ARTA has steadfastly maintained its identity as an art cinema since its establishment in 1913.

Libraries

The Central University Library
"Lucian Blaga"

The Central University Library "Lucian Blaga" is a pivotal institution in the national education system, emphasizing intellectual training through education, knowledge, and research. With its encyclopedic profile, museum character, and valuable collections, the library serves the information needs of the local and national academic community.

Nestled in a city that values multiculturalism, the library, alongside Babeș-Bolyai University, actively promotes diverse cultural values. Noteworthy is its extensive Hungarian and German book collection, among Romania's largest, contributing significantly to the history and culture of Transylvania and Central Europe.

Boasting a diverse collection exceeding 4 million volumes, including 497,000 periodicals, the library stands as a crucial resource for academic education in the region. Facilitating access to prestigious international scientific databases, it collaborates seamlessly with faculty libraries and cultural centers.

The Central University Library provides 2,473 seats across over 60 reading rooms, enhancing the academic experience and underscoring its dedication to fostering a vibrant and knowledge-driven community.

Performing Arts

The National Theater

The National Theatre in Cluj-Napoca is a cultural landmark renowned for its rich heritage and architectural splendor since 1919. With a diverse range of classical and contemporary productions, it offers a unique cultural experience, showcasing the talents of local artists.

Beyond performances, the theatre engages in community initiatives, fostering a sense of connection. Its involvement in cultural events and festivals further contributes to Cluj's vibrant cultural scene, making it a must-visit for those seeking artistic enrichment.

Reactor -
a hub for creative experiments

Mainly known for their theater endeavours, Reactor - a hub for creative experiments - is a dynamic cultural center in Cluj-Napoca, embracing alternative artistic experiences. Established in 2014, it houses an independent theater, an exhibition venue, and a small library.

With a focus on supporting young artists, contemporary playwriting, and fostering social awareness, Reactor has become a catalyst for Cluj's creative energies. Open to people and ideas, it encourages artistic experimentation and collaboration within the local, national, and international artistic community.

The belief in a thriving cultural environment as crucial for city development underscores Reactor's commitment to freedom of expression and enriching the quality of life in Cluj.

The National Opera

The Cluj-Napoca National Opera, established in 1919, stands as a cultural cornerstone, weaving a rich tapestry into the city's artistic heritage. Its architectural elegance, a testament to both history and art, enhances the overall experience, providing a visually captivating backdrop to a diverse array of productions. From classical operas to contemporary works and ballets, the National Opera caters to a broad audience, offering a glimpse into the city's cultural vibrancy.

This cultural institution not only showcases the talents of skilled performers but also actively engages with the community through educational programs, workshops, and collaborations with local artists. Beyond the stage, the National Opera is a focal point for various cultural events and collaborations, contributing to Cluj's diverse cultural landscape. Whether attending for the sheer artistic excellence, historical ambiance, or community involvement, a visit to the Cluj-Napoca National Opera promises a captivating and enriching cultural experience.
